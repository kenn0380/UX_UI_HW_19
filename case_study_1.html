How might we improve the UI of and brand identity of BIA’s website to see increased publicity and event popularity?

The Problem

We assumed that the organization primarily valued donations and volunteer registration on the BIA website.

After interviewing the stakeholder, however, we discovered that only $7k of a $5.5 million annual budget comes from donations. We needed to iterate our problem statement to adjust for the new key-performance-indicator: How might we streamline volunteer registration user-flows to result in higher volunteer rates, more popular events, and greater publicity?

The User

In usability testing, interviewees commented that a site might be considered trustworthy if it had a clean and polished design. Furthermore, without seeing that an organization is active, impactful, and legitimate, users will not have gained enough trust to volunteer for them.

""""

The Solution

We developed a streamlined user-flow that enabled users to sign up for an event with less than three clicks. This will ensure that users don't get lost during the process, and increase the likelihood that they'll folllow it through until the end.

We also reorganized the information architecture to allow users to identify the ligitimacy of the organization, and also a Facebook feed demonstrating how the're currently active.
